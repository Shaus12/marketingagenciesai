# How to Write Captions & Social Copy

This file defines how the founder's voice works on each platform. Read before writing any social content.

**Model:** Use Opus 4.6 for initial drafts. Sonnet for variations.

---

## the founder's Voice (Non-Negotiable)

Every piece of copy must sound like the founder wrote it himself. Not an agency. Not AI. Not a marketer.

### How the founder sounds:
- **First person.** "I" not "we." Even when talking about YourBrand.
- **Short sentences.** One thought per line. Let the line breaks do the work.
- **Specific.** Real numbers, real names, real examples. Never "many users" — always "2,307 prompt generations."
- **Honest.** Acknowledges what doesn't work. "I built 25 apps before 3 made money" is more the founder than "I've helped thousands succeed."
- **Slightly Dutch-direct.** Gets to the point without softening. Not rude — just efficient.
- **Peer, not guru.** "I've been there" not "I'll teach you." Fellow builder, not teacher.
- **No hype.** Never "game-changing" / "revolutionary" / "unlock your potential." Just: "Here's what it does. Here's what it costs. Here's what you get."

### Quick test:
Read the copy out loud. Does it sound like a person talking to one friend? Or does it sound like a marketing department wrote it? If the latter — rewrite.

## Platform-Specific Voice

### Instagram (Caption)
- 3-8 lines. Short paragraphs.
- First line = the hook. Everything after the fold ("...more") is a bonus.
- End with a question or a challenge — invite saves/shares.
- NO hashtags in the caption body. If using, put in first comment.
- NO "link in bio" (unless linking somewhere specific). It sounds desperate.
- Emojis: 0-2 max. Functional only (→ or ✓). Not decorative (🚀🔥💪).

**Example of good Instagram caption:**
```
Building is easy in 2026.
Getting paid is the hard part.

I built 25 apps before 3 made money.
The difference wasn't the code.
It wasn't the design.
It wasn't the tech stack.

It was knowing what to build,
who actually wanted it,
and what to charge.

That's the part nobody teaches.
And that's the part that actually matters.

What's the biggest thing stopping you from earning from what you build?
```

**Example of bad Instagram caption:**
```
🚀 Want to make money with AI? Here's the thing - building AI products is easier than ever! 💡 But monetization is KEY. 🔑

I've learned SO much from my journey, and I want to share my top tips with you!

1. Validate first ✅
2. Find your niche 🎯
3. Price correctly 💰

Drop a 🔥 if you agree! Link in bio for more! 👆

#AI #SaaS #BuildInPublic #IndieHacker #NoCode
```

### LinkedIn (Post)
- Professional but personal. Not corporate.
- Open with a hook line. LinkedIn truncates after 2 lines — the hook must earn the "see more" click.
- Use data and specific experiences. LinkedIn audience respects evidence.
- Paragraph breaks matter — wall of text = skip.
- End with a perspective, not a pitch.
- No emojis. No hashtags in the body (optional at the end, max 3).

**Example of good LinkedIn post:**
```
I looked at my data last week.

2,307 people used the AI tools I built.
5 people completed the course I spent 3 months creating.

That's a 460:1 ratio.

So I deleted the course as the main experience
and rebuilt everything around the tools.

The lesson isn't about courses vs tools.
It's about what people actually do
vs what we think they want.

We assumed people wanted to learn.
They wanted to do.

Every product decision should start with
usage data, not assumptions.
```

### Twitter/X (Tweet)
- One thought. Maximum punch.
- Under 200 characters is ideal. Under 280 if needed.
- No threads (unless specifically asked for).
- No emojis. No hashtags.
- The tweet should feel like a thought that interrupted someone's day.

**Examples:**
- "Everyone can build with AI now. Almost nobody makes money from it. The gap isn't technical."
- "I built 25 apps before 3 made money. The difference was never the code."
- "The era of video courses is over. 2,307 tool uses vs 5 lesson completions. People want to do, not watch."

### Meta Ads (Primary Text)
- See AD_RULES.md for full guidelines
- PAS structure: Pain → Agitate → Solution (the webinar)
- Never mention YourBrand or the product
- End with clean CTA: "↓ Register below — it is free"
- First 125 characters are everything (mobile truncation)

## What Makes a Caption Bad

These are signs the copy needs rewriting:

1. **Starts with a question everyone answers "yes" to.** "Want to make money with AI?" — obviously yes, that's why they follow you. No tension.
2. **Uses filler phrases.** "Here's the thing..." / "I want to share..." / "Let me tell you..." — just say it.
3. **Lists without insight.** "5 tips for building AI products: 1. Validate 2. Build 3. Launch..." — every creator posts this.
4. **Ends with "link in bio."** Unless there's a specific reason, this sounds like every other pitch.
5. **Too many emojis.** 🚀🔥💡🎯 = "I'm trying too hard to seem exciting"
6. **AI-generated patterns.** Phrases like "In today's rapidly evolving landscape" or "Let's dive in" or "Without further ado" — dead giveaways.
7. **Hashtag spam.** #AI #SaaS #BuildInPublic #NoCode #IndieHacker — LinkedIn 2019 energy.

## The Editing Process

1. **Draft** — Write the raw thought. Don't worry about polish.
2. **Cut** — Remove every word that doesn't add meaning. "I think that" → cut. "Really" → cut. "Actually" → cut.
3. **Sharpen** — Make every sentence do one job. If a sentence does two things, split it.
4. **Voice check** — Read it out loud as the founder. Does it sound like him or like a content marketer?
5. **Platform check** — Is this right for the platform? A LinkedIn post isn't a tweet isn't an Instagram caption.
6. **Screenshot test** — Would someone screenshot this specific post? If not, it's not sharp enough.

## Lessons Learned

*Update with real performance data:*

- Short, direct posts outperform long explanations on Instagram
- Data-driven LinkedIn posts (with real numbers) get 3-5x more engagement than opinion posts
- Tweets that state an uncomfortable truth get more engagement than advice tweets
- "I built 25 apps" personal story format consistently drives engagement across all platforms
- Ending with a question increases comments by ~40% on Instagram
