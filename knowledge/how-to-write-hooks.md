# How to Write Hooks

This file defines what makes a hook work. Read before generating any hooks for ads, Reels, Shorts, or social posts.

**Model:** Use Opus 4.6. Hook writing is the highest-leverage marketing task — a great hook 10x outperforms a mediocre one.

---

## What a Hook Does

A hook has one job: **earn the next 3 seconds.**

It does NOT sell. It does NOT explain. It does NOT introduce. It makes someone stop scrolling because they MUST know what comes next.

---

## The 5 Hook Patterns That Work For Us (Ranked by Data)

### 1. Pain Call-Out (Best performer)
Name the exact situation they're in right now.

**Why it works:** The audience feels instantly seen. They can't scroll past something that describes their life.

**Formula:** [Specific scenario they're living] + [emotional weight]

**Examples that worked:**
- "You're 200 prompts deep. The app was supposed to take 2 hours but it's been 2 days." (96K views)
- "You shipped it. Nobody came." (v9 ad — high engagement)
- "90% of AI builders never make a single euro." (€X.XX CPL — best ad ever)

**How to find these:** Look for moments in transcripts where the founder describes a frustrating experience. The more specific, the better. "Building is hard" = generic. "You're 200 prompts deep and something keeps breaking" = specific.

### 2. Contrarian Challenge
Say the opposite of what they expect.

**Why it works:** Pattern interrupt. Everyone says "learn to code" — when someone says "stop coding," the brain flags it as important.

**Formula:** "Stop [thing everyone does]. [What to do instead]."

**Examples that worked:**
- "Stop Vibe Coding (Do This Instead)" — 96K views, #1 video
- "Stop watching AI tutorials. Seriously." — running ad
- "Stop Learning n8n RIGHT NOW" — 69K views, #2 video

**Important:** The contrarian take must be GENUINE, not clickbait. There must be a real insight behind it. "Stop" without substance = manipulative.

### 3. Specific Number
A stat or data point that reframes the situation.

**Why it works:** Numbers are concrete in a sea of vague advice. They signal "this person has real data, not just opinions."

**Formula:** [Surprising number] + [what it means for them]

**Examples that worked:**
- "90% of AI builders never make a single euro" — €X.XX CPL
- "I built 25 apps. 3 made money." — v9 ad
- "Trust in AI-generated code dropped from 40% to 29% in one year"
- "2,307 people used the tools. 5 completed the course."

**Rule:** The number must be SURPRISING. "1,000 people use our product" = boring. "2,307 people used the tools vs 5 who watched the course" = shocking contrast.

### 4. Personal Confession
the founder sharing a real failure or uncomfortable truth.

**Why it works:** Vulnerability + specificity = trust. People are tired of polished gurus.

**Formula:** "I [failed/struggled at thing] before [specific result]."

**Examples:**
- "I built 25 apps before 3 made money. The difference wasn't the code."
- "I looked at my data. 2,307 people used the tools. 5 completed the course. So I rebuilt everything."

**Rule:** Must be REAL. Must have a LESSON. Confession without insight = just complaining.

### 5. Dollar/Value Anchor
A specific money amount that makes the opportunity real.

**Why it works:** Abstract "make money with AI" = ignorable. Specific "$500/month from one product" = imaginable.

**Formula:** [Who pays] + [how much] + [for what]

**Examples:**
- "Dental offices pay €500 every single month for this"
- "3 clients × €500/mo = €1,500 recurring"
- "What if your next weekend project made you 500 euros every single month?"

**Rule:** The dollar amount must be BELIEVABLE. Not "$100K in 30 days" (scammy). But "$500/month from one AI product" (achievable, specific).

## What Doesn't Work as a Hook

These patterns consistently underperform or get zero leads:

- **Vanity metrics**: "55 products shipped" — €21 spent, 0 leads. Nobody cares how many things you built.
- **Feature announcements**: "We just added a new tool!" — not a pain, not interesting
- **Questions without tension**: "Want to build an AI product?" — yes, obviously, that's why they're here
- **Generic benefits**: "Learn how to monetize AI" — every creator says this
- **"Hey, I'm the founder..."** — instant scroll-past. Never introduce yourself in a hook.
- **Hype words**: "This is INSANE" / "Mind-blowing" / "Game-changer" — triggers the "this is an ad" filter

---

## Hook Quality Checklist

Before using any hook, it must pass ALL of these:

- [ ] **Would I stop scrolling for this?** — Be honest. Not "would someone" — would YOU.
- [ ] **Is this specific?** — Does it contain a number, a scenario, or a named pain? Or is it vague?
- [ ] **Does it sound like the founder?** — Direct, slightly blunt, honest. Not salesy, not guru-ish.
- [ ] **Does it create a gap?** — After reading it, do I NEED to know what comes next?
- [ ] **Can it stand alone?** — Without any context, does it still hit?
- [ ] **Is it under 15 words?** — The best hooks are short. If it takes 5 seconds to read, it's too long.

## Lessons Learned

*Update this section with real performance data:*

- Pain call-out hooks produce our lowest CPL (€1.21 vs €3.50+ for other patterns)
- Contrarian hooks drive both YouTube views AND ad performance — rare crossover
- Dollar-value hooks get 6.1-6.7% engagement on YouTube
- "Stop doing X" is the most reliable hook pattern across all platforms
- Generic proof/numbers ("55 products shipped") creates curiosity but not conversion — people click but don't register. The hook must lead to a PAIN, not a brag.
