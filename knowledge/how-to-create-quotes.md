# How to Create Quote Cards

This file defines what makes a quote card worth posting. Read this before generating any static text content.

**Model:** Use Opus 4.6 for selection and refinement. The thinking matters more than the generation.

---

## What a Quote Card Is NOT

- It is NOT a random sentence from a transcript
- It is NOT a generic motivational quote ("believe in yourself")
- It is NOT a summary of the video's topic
- It is NOT something ChatGPT would generate if you asked "give me 3 quotes about AI"

## What a Quote Card IS

A quote card is a **single thought that changes how someone sees their situation.**

After reading it, they should feel one of these:
1. **"That's exactly my problem"** — they feel seen
2. **"I never thought of it that way"** — their mental model shifts
3. **"I need to save this"** — it's reference-worthy, not just interesting
4. **"I need to send this to [person]"** — it has sharing momentum

If the quote doesn't trigger at least one of these — don't use it.

---

## The Anatomy of a Great Quote Card

### Structure
The best quote cards follow one of these patterns:

**1. The Reframe**
Takes something they think is true and flips it.
- "You don't have a building problem. You have a selling problem."
- "The hard part isn't code. It's knowing what to build, who wants it, and what to charge."
- "ChatGPT is a kitchen. YourBrand is a personal chef."

**2. The Uncomfortable Truth**
Says what they're afraid to admit to themselves.
- "Every prompt you fire without a plan adds something you don't fully understand."
- "You shipped it. Nobody came. And it wasn't your product's fault."
- "You've been learning how to build for 6 months. You still haven't sold anything."

**3. The Specific Proof**
A real number or personal experience that grounds the claim.
- "I built 25 apps. 3 made money. The difference wasn't the code."
- "2,307 people used the tools. 5 completed the course. So I rebuilt everything."
- "Trust in AI-generated code dropped from 40% to 29% in one year."

**4. The Sharp Distinction**
A clean before/after or this-vs-that comparison.
- "The vibe coder works backwards from whatever AI produces. The engineer works forwards from a clear picture."
- "Information is free. Execution is what you pay for."
- "Watching tutorials is preparing. Using tools is doing."

### What to avoid:
- **Vague wisdom**: "Success comes from doing the right things" — meaningless
- **Too long**: If it's more than 2 sentences, it's not a quote card — it's a paragraph
- **No tension**: "AI tools are really powerful" — who disagrees? There's no edge here
- **Requires context**: "That's why step 7 is so important" — means nothing alone
- **Sounds like AI wrote it**: Overly polished, symmetrical, uses words like "leverage" or "unlock"

## Visual Design Rules

Every quote card uses the YourBrand brand system:

- **Background**: Dark (#0a0a0a) or brand template with lime corner glows
- **Text**: Bold white sans-serif (Montserrat/Inter, heavy weight)
- **Accent**: Key phrase highlighted in lime (#YOUR_ACCENT)
- **Format**: 1080x1080 (Instagram feed) or 1080x1350 (4:5 portrait)
- **Maximum text**: 15-20 words on the image. The caption adds context.
- **Attribution**: Small "— the founder / YourBrand" at the bottom in gray

The quote must be readable in 2 seconds on a phone screen. If you have to squint — it's too long.

---

## The Caption Is Half the Post

The image stops the scroll. The caption delivers the value.

A quote card caption should:
1. **Expand on the quote** — give the context or story behind it (3-5 lines)
2. **End with a question or challenge** — invite engagement
3. **Not repeat the quote** — they already read it in the image
4. **Sound like the founder** — first person, direct, honest

Example:
> Image: "I built 25 apps. 3 made money."
>
> Caption: "The other 22 weren't bad products. They were products built for nobody in particular. I skipped validation on every single one. Built what I thought was cool instead of what someone would pay for. The 3 that worked? I asked people what they needed before I wrote a single prompt. That's the difference."

## Selection Process

When analyzing a video for quote cards:

1. Read the full transcript
2. Find 5-10 candidate lines that could be quotes
3. For each, ask: "Would someone screenshot this?" If not — cut it.
4. For survivors, ask: "Does this sound like the founder or like generic AI advice?" If generic — cut it.
5. Refine the wording — the founder speaks in short, direct sentences. Not academic. Not motivational. Just real.
6. You should end with 1-3 quote cards per video. Not every video has quotable moments. That's fine.

**It's better to create 1 great quote card than 3 mediocre ones.**

## Lessons Learned

*Update this section as we learn what performs:*

- Reframe quotes ("You don't have X problem, you have Y problem") get the highest saves on Instagram
- Stats with a twist ("25 apps, 3 made money") get the highest shares
- Quotes longer than 20 words lose impact on mobile
- Lime-highlighted key words increase readability and brand recognition
- Quote cards about selling/monetization outperform quote cards about building
